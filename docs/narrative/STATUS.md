Narrative covers the story content of the game: the Saturn anomaly storyline (the central v1 mission arc), the two-eras worldbuilding (the prior wave of exploration 2020s-2040s and the current era 2050-2100), Tier A artifact placement, in-game text, and character writing.

The high-level design is locked in docs/PHASE_0_ARTIFACT_LIST.md (commit 037).

## Decisions captured

**The Saturn anomaly:** two named astronauts from the prior wave are at a Saturn station, orbiting Saturn (not the icy moon directly). They are in critical condition, having been scoping an icy moon for habitability. They are rescuable. Successful rescue makes them veteran crew, carrying forward into the rest of the game.

**The icy moon:** has a subsurface ocean. Visible jets/plumes erupt from the moon (always visible, visible from orbit). This serves as visual interest and narrative hook — evidence of subsurface activity, the kind of thing the astronauts would have been studying.

**The prior wave's silence:** unresolved in v1. Players know it ended; they don't know why. That ambiguity is deliberate.

**Tier A artifacts** discoverable throughout the home system: Voyager-equivalent probes, ISS-equivalent station, Apollo sites, Mars landers, the inherited rover at the home moon, the Saturn station with its astronauts, one distant interstellar-medium destination.

**Two-eras worldbuilding through environmental storytelling.** Not expository text. Artifacts at locations, log entries left by prior-wave crew, equipment branding, base architecture style.

**Character writing.** Two starting crew (named). Two rescuable Saturn astronauts (named). Other crew from random-name pool. In v1, characters have names and possibly portraits. Backstories and personalities are post-launch additions.

**Logbooks discoverable.** Player may find log entries from prior-wave crew. Pieces of the story emerge through these discoveries. Specific log content not yet written.
